Summary

Today's session will take the form as a seminar with a topic on 'ideation'. Ideation is the creative process of developing ideas. You will use this session as a group to understand the various elements of the UX process with a focus on people and technology. You will later revisit ideation individually for assignment two.

Aim

This seminar aims to provide you with an understanding of the various UX problems acting as a foundation to begin exploring what area you would like to discover in your assignment. As a group you will ideate around what interests you in UX and what types of projects you might want to design for.

Platforms/services are environments where software and hardware exist. For example, a VR headset might use Horizon Worlds as its platform. Alternatively, a chatbot could be considered a platform/service that exists are part of something much greater.

  • Spend 10 minutes jotting down what platforms you are interested in designing for (use this activity as freely and as flexibly as you wish)

  • One person read aloud the post-its and encouraged discussion (group post-its that are similar/the same)

Activity 6. Solution paradigm

  • Using the solution paradigm, gather everyone's input from activities 3, 4 and 5

  • One person read aloud the post-its and encouraged discussion (group post-its that are similar/the same)

If physical, take a photo of the final post-it contributions and share in the Miro board

Activity 7. What is ideation?

10-15 minutes

Ideation is a creative process that involves using methods to assist with the formalising of an idea. Ideation is used to address a problem to create solutions. The purpose of ideation is to come up with many ideas to solve one problem (your first idea is not the best idea and there is no bad idea).

You conduct ideation sessions to generate new angles and approaches to problem-solving. The techniques (methods) used in ideation are there to enable you to think differently and innovate novel approaches to often wicked (complex) problems.

It is important to remember that you should only ideate once you have clearly defined your problem.

  • Watch 'Brainstorming and other ideation techniques at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xXsHI_VlhmY

Ideation is all about time constraints, you will see from these sessions that every activity is finely timed - this is to ensure you train your mind to think quickly and promote quantity.
