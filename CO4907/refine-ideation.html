CO4907: Prototyping and Evaluation

Lab: Ideation (refine)

Remember labs often follow on from the lectures. If you are stuck you should review your lecture notes, slides or recording as essential information is contained in the topics you learn week on week. Practical work (labs and coursework) will require you to review past lectures and online materials, and books as you will be required to assimilate and ground this knowledge over time.

 

Summary

By now you should have identified the problem space you are attempting to explore in this module. You will start to generate solutions to the problem - this is the process known as divergent thinking. You will begin to narrow down these solutions, ready for prototyping. By the end of the session, you will have decided upon where your focus will be - convergent thinking.

Aim

This session aims to further your knowledge and understanding of the ideation process. You will begin to realise your problem space and begin to ideate possible solutions. By understanding your users in more depth, you start to appreciate what issues your users have with a particular problem. So far you have been thinking about problems and solutions using texts, by the end of the session you will have visualised solutions ready for building on the creative process next week.

 

Objectives

This session aims to achieve the following objective(s) below:

O.1: Begin the process of formalising ideas to solve problems

O.2: Practice methods for ideation - shifting from text to visual communication

 

Intended Learning Outcome

This lab intends to contribute towards achieving ILOs 1 and 3. "Design appropriate solutions to real-world problems through prototyping" and "Demonstrate an awareness of industry practices following a UX strategy and process"

 

Introduction

By now, you should have reframed your problem. You will have a collection of HMWs that have been decided on using the 'How, Now, Wow Matrix'. Your decisions should have been made about the area you are wanting to explore. From here you will have created an initial mind map to set out what are the knowns/unknowns of the problem space identified.

If not, do not continue with this activity. Please go back to the previous weeks as the following activities assume you have the problem space identified. In today's session, you will start to understand your users.

Activities

Each week through completing the lab activities you will build up all the required knowledge to contribute towards the prototype assessment, you are however expected to continue this through independent learning. In some instances, the activities will highlight what you should continue through independent learning.

Remember: Each week you will be building on existing work, as it is your space you might want to use titles so you can remember what segments of work relate to which part of the process.

 

Activity 1. Art Museum

0-30 minutes

  • Display your progress so far

  • Discuss your idea

  • Collect feedback and start to further develop your idea

     

 

Activity 2. User Flow

30-60 minutes

You are now ready to start to extrapolate your thinking by creating journeys. These will help you when it comes to storyboarding your solution.

  • Copy and paste the user journey template into your space

  • Start by breaking down your problem space into stages (you can have as many stages as you like, three have already been created for you)

  • Build up the journey by thinking about the following areas

    1. Actions

    2. Touchpoints

    3. Insights

    4. Feelings

    5. Opportunities

In this activity you are expanding your idea to situate it within a context of the end-user and their environment, social sphere and technological landscape.

 

 

Break

60-70 minutes

 

Activity 3. The idea on a Post-it

70-80 minutes

  • On a Post-it within your space

    • Write a short description of your idea (solution)

    • Write who your target audience is

    • Write what the innovative aspect of your work is

    • Write what the value/impact of your work has on the world/community/discipline

       

Activity 4. Storyboard

80-110 minutes

Use the Storyformer template and build up a narrative/script for your solution.

Creating the storyline

For the Rising Action and the Climax, use the selected aspects from Step 1 and imagine what situations can best illustrate the value of those aspects for your persona.

Remember that you are telling a story about your persona. Do not only describe your solution idea but indicate what the persona does and thinks. You can check the Freitag's Pyramid

You don't need to fill in the storyline in order. Sometimes it is easier to start with the Goal Achievement and then continue with the Current situation and Trigger.

  • Copy and paste the Storyformer template into your space

  • Complete the sections by writing your responses on the Post-it notes provided

  • Using the Storyboard template, start by copying and pasting the post-its from the 'Storyformer' and positioning them onto the plot diagram within the appropriate event

  • Create a 9-part storyboard that has a beginning, middle and end to explain your problem, user, and solution (giving as much context as you can). It would help if you did this through visual means i.e., sketching

    • You might want to do this on paper first and upload it onto Miro as one whole storyboard

    • or capture each element of the story and upload it into the table provided

  • Create a User Story based on the story you've created
